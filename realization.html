Issues and Solutions

During the course of our project we faced various problems that we were able to correct. Thus, we will list some of them.

Encountered Issues


1) Implementation of the cars                                           
   - The implementation of the car physics was complex, especially considering the numerous variables impacting the behavior of a car. It was necessary to manage the weight, speed, engine horsepower, maximum torque, steering angle and speed, braking force and speed, engine inertia and center of gravity height. After the implementation of the physics of the cars, we realized that the models of the cars we had did not allow its good
function.
  - Another problem was the sound design of the car. Several elements in a car produce sound and do not have the same behavior depending on the weight and speed of the vehicle or the power of the engine.

2) Artificial Intelligence
  - When implementing our AI, we have many different problems. To begin with, the autonomous cars did not want to go through the right places. They would turn around on the starting line and drive through walls or between studs and stop for no reason in the middle of the track.
  - The second problem consisted in an unexpected and systematic stop in the middle of the circuit.

3) Music Implementation
  - For the menus we preferred to create several scenes for the sub-menus accessible by the main menu, rather than a single scene which is transformed at the press of a button on the main menu.
the press of a button on the menu. While convenient for individual scripts for each game mode and sub-menu, this complicated the implementation of the music. A way had to be found to prevent the music from stopping each time a new menu was loaded.
  - A subsequent problem was that although the music did not stop, every time a menu was opened another instance of the music would start.
   - Finally a last problem occurred when implementing the
music. The menu music kept playing over the race music.

Deployed Solutions


1) Implementation of the cars
  - First, we had to modify the prefabs of the cars, among others their rigibody which prevented the wheels from turning and from considering that they touched the ground. Following this, it was also necessary to modify the wheels of the cars. This was not done before in order to drive the axle and synchronize the steering. After finding this problem on one car, it was necessary to apply it to the others, which also explains why the cars have the same physics for now. However, the cars as well as the collisions are functional.
  - We had to manage the gear shifting, the engine noise whose pitch and volume changed according to the speed or the type of the vehicle (a pick-up has a different noise than a Supercar). With the gear shift there was also the noise of the turbocharger or supercharger to manage. In the end we found some parameters that allowed the car to have a good behavior and sound.

2) Artificial Intelligence
  - To solve this problem, we figured out how the NavMesh works and then learned to separate the different layers of the track, what the car can drive on and what the obstacles are. The NavMesh is a layer that is placed on top of the circuit and defines an area on which the autonomous cars can drive and calculate their route. After that, we recalculated the NavMesh and put back studs or barriers where needed.
  - To figure out how to solve this problem, we tested several things, such as whether the car's direction was the right one, and then whether the checkpoints worked properly. After a long and fruitless search, we found that the problem was related to the absence of components in the car, allowing to count the collisions with the checkpoints. So we added this component and changed the way to define a trajectory. And after this work, the autonomous cars arrive at the end of the circuit.

3) Music implementation
 - For this we had to make sure that the music is part of the DontDestroyOnLoad elements, which allows that once the music is loaded, it will remain accessible even after changing scene. We then had to make a reference to the music in the script and tell it not to stop when a scene was changed.
    - To remedy this we had to specify in the script that any new music in a menu scene should not start if another was already playing.             - We then had to add in the script the case where when we were running, we had to put the SetActive property, which allows to activate or deactivate an item. For example, if we entered the race, we had to disable the menu music and enable the race music.
